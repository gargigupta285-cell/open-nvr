# OpenNVR Trademark & Branding Policy

The OpenNVR name, logo, and the "Powered by OpenNVR" badge are
trademarks of the OpenNVR project. The code license (AGPL/Apache) gives
you rights to the *code* — this policy governs the *name*. Keeping the
two separate is what lets the code stay genuinely open while the brand
stays trustworthy.

## Always fine (no permission needed)

- Truthful, nominative statements: "built on OpenNVR", "compatible
  with OpenNVR", "an app for the OpenNVR App Store".
- Unmodified builds referred to as OpenNVR.
- Community content: posts, talks, tutorials, package names like
  `opennvr-<something>` for apps/adapters targeting the platform.

## The "Powered by OpenNVR" badge

- **Free to use** by anyone shipping a product or service that runs
  substantially unmodified OpenNVR under the AGPL. We encourage it.
- **Required** for OpenNVR Commercial License holders (OEM/ISV), in
  the product UI and marketing materials — unless the white-label
  tier is purchased. This requirement lives in the commercial
  contract, not in the code license.
- Badge assets ship in `app/public/`; don't distort, recolor, or pair
  them with claims of endorsement.

## Not allowed without written permission

- Naming a product, company, domain, or hardware appliance "OpenNVR"
  or something confusingly similar.
- Using the name/logo on a **modified** distribution in a way that
  suggests it is the official OpenNVR (fork freely — under a
  different name).
- Implying certification. "OpenNVR Certified" is granted only through
  the commercial program.

Questions or permission requests: open a "Trademark" issue on GitHub.
